About

I am a PhD candidate in the School of Built Environment at Massey University, Auckland. My research focuses on occupant behaviour during emergencies and occupant safety in buildings.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a lecturer in digital spatial design at Haikou University of Economics in China (2021–2025), where I taught courses on VR/AR, Digital Twin development, and construction economics. I also have professional experience in architectural design, interior design, and business development across New Zealand, the UK, and China.

My supervisory team includes Dr Don Samarasinghe, Dr Harold Aquino, and Dr Fei Ying.

Research Interests

  • Occupant behaviour and safety during building emergencies
  • Digital technologies in the built environment (VR/AR, IoT, BIM, Digital Twins)
  • Human factors influencing emergency response in buildings
  • Digital spatial design and immersive learning environments
  • Application of emerging technologies in construction and building management
